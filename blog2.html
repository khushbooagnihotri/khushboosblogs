My Journey to become a google developers student club Lead

At the end of my freshman year, I got an email regarding the interviews for being a DSC Core Team Member in my college, G. H. Raisoni College of Engineering. Many people were interviewed, but only five people were selected and I was one of them!

Google Developer Student Clubs are community groups for college and university students interested in Google developer technologies. Students from all undergraduate or graduate programs with an interest in growing as a developer can join DSC. By joining a DSC, students can grow their knowledge in a peer-to-peer learning environment and build solutions for local businesses and their communities. Developers Student Clubs is one of the Developer Community programs powered by Google Developers. It was launched in India in 2017 and DSC’s are now in 68+ countries with 860+ groups.

As a core team member, I helped in organizing many events and workshops like 30 Days of Google Cloud, Hands-on sessions on UI & UX, and also collaborated with other DSC’s in DSC WOW.

You can visit here to know more about my experience as a Core Team Member at DSC GHRCE.

I like to challenge myself and explore new opportunities so when the applications for GDSC Leads opened in April this year, I did not waste any time and jumped on this opportunity. The former Lead of my club was supportive and guided me through the whole process. I was suggested to apply as soon as I could and be careful while doing so.

The application consisted of a few sections, after filling in the basic details three community questions were compulsory, and at last a recorded video of the applicant not longer than 2 minutes, describing who you are and why you should become a Lead. I made sure that all my answers were personalized and were nearly the same word count as specified. You can also get a senior or a family member to review your answers and check them once for grammar And plagiarism issues too. My previous Lead, Shreyas Malewar also provided me with a referral for my application.

After applying, I received an email in May, informing me to schedule a video call with a representative for the further selection process. I chose a time slot I was comfortable with and gave the interview a week later. The call went on for about 30 minutes, and though I was a bit nervous, the interviewer made sure I was comfortable before we started. The questions mainly revolved around why I should be the Lead and who I am as an individual. Since I was a core team member before, I was asked about my experience and my role in the team. Make sure to highlight your contributions and skills, I didn’t shy away from flaunting a blog program that I initiated and managed as an editor in DSC GHRCE, mentioned a project I was working on and the technologies I used for it, and also mention your past leadership experiences like I was the student council president in my first year and had a good experience in organizing events.

My interview went well but still, I had some doubts as I thought that I could have said something different, but when I received my selection email on a random Thursday afternoon in July, I was ecstatic and relieved.

I’m excited to begin a new chapter as the GDSC lead of my campus and impact more students as I move ahead!
